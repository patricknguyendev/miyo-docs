A savings goal tracks progress toward a longer-term finish line. It is separate from the category targets used to plan recurring spending.

## Create a goal

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Savings goals">
    Open **Plan your money** and select **Savings goals**, then choose **New Savings Goal**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the finish line">
    Enter a **Goal name** and **Savings target**. Add an optional target date if you want Miyo to calculate the monthly pace needed to finish on time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Personalize the goal">
    Choose an icon and color so the goal is easy to recognize.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose household access">
    If you have a household, turn on **Share with Partner** when they should see your progress. Choose **Make this a joint goal** when both partners should contribute.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the goal">
    Select **Create Goal**. New savings start at \$0 until you record a contribution.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Record progress

Open the goal and select **Contribute**. Enter the amount and an optional note, such as “Birthday money” or “July transfer.”

<Warning>
  Recording a contribution updates Miyo's progress only. Miyo is read-only and does not transfer money into a bank or savings account.
</Warning>

## Pause, resume, edit, or delete

- **Pause** stops the goal from being treated as active without removing its history.
- **Resume** returns a paused goal to active planning.
- **Edit Goal** changes its name, target, date, icon, or color.
- **Delete** removes the goal; review the confirmation carefully when contribution history matters.

## Personal, shared, and joint goals

| Goal type | Who can see it | Who can contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Personal | You | You |
| Shared with partner | You and your partner | You; your partner can see progress |
| Joint | You and your partner | Both household members |
