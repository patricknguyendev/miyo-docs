---
title: "Add a manual account"
description: "Track an account without connecting it to a financial institution."
icon: "pencil-line"
---

A manual account gives you full control over the balance and activity. Use one for cash, an unsupported institution, or any account you do not want to link.

## Add the account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the new-account form">
    Open **Accounts** and select **Add Account** or **Add manually**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the account details">
    Add an **Account Name**, optional **Institution Name**, **Account Type**, current balance, and three-letter currency code.

    Supported account types include checking, savings, credit card, investment, loan, mortgage, cash, and other.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the correct balance sign">
    Enter money you own as a positive balance. Enter debt, including credit cards and loans, as a negative balance or zero.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add debt details when useful">
    For a credit card, loan, or mortgage, enter the available **Credit Limit** and **APR (%)** fields when they appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the account">
    Select **Create Account**. The account appears in **Accounts** and can receive manual or imported transactions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Miyo does not refresh a manual balance. Update it yourself when a statement arrives or when you want account totals to reflect recent activity.
</Warning>

## Edit or close a manual account

Open the account, then select its edit action. You can change its name, institution, balance, currency, credit details, notes, or status.

| Status | Use it when |
| --- | --- |
| **Active** | You still use and track the account |
| **Inactive** | You want to keep it without treating it as current |
| **Closed** | The financial account is closed but its history should remain |

<Tip>
  Closing an account is usually safer than deleting financial history. It preserves context for older transactions and reports.
</Tip>
