---
title: "Change household account visibility"
description: "Set each Miyo account to Private, Shared, or Hidden, preview what your household partner can see, and review visibility changes before sharing."
---

Miyo applies household visibility per account. Open **Privacy Center** to review every account before relying on a shared dashboard or report.

## Choose a visibility state

| State | You can see it | Your partner can see it | Included in your dashboard and reports |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Private** | Yes | No | Yes |
| **Shared** | Yes | Yes, including shared account activity | Yes |
| **Hidden** | No in normal finance views | No | No |

<Warning>
  **Hidden** is broader than **Private**. Hidden accounts are excluded from your own normal dashboard totals and reports as well as household views.
</Warning>

## Change an account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Privacy Center">
    Open **Privacy Center** and review the Shared, Private, and Hidden counts at the top.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the account">
    Confirm the account name, institution, type, and balance so you do not change a similarly named account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the new state">
    Select **Private**, **Shared**, or **Hidden**, then confirm the change if Miyo asks.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview the partner view">
    Use the partner-view preview to check what the new household view will reveal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the audit log">
    The **Privacy audit log** records visibility changes so you can see which account changed and when.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Goals and debt use separate sharing rules

- A personal savings goal stays yours.
- **Share with Partner** lets a partner see a goal's progress.
- A joint goal lets both partners contribute.
- Shared debt can reveal payment timing for coordination while keeping APR and payoff strategy private.

<Note>
  Account visibility controls do not change ownership at the financial institution and do not grant transaction or money-movement authority.
</Note>
