You can start using Miyo by yourself. A household and bank connection are optional, so choose the setup that matches how you want to work today.

## Before you begin

You need a Miyo account and access to the email address you used to sign up. If you plan to import data, also have either a CSV export from your bank or a YNAB export ready.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add your first financial account">
    Open **Accounts**, then choose one path:

    - **Connect Bank** to link an available institution through Plaid.
    - **Import CSV** to create an account and upload transactions from a file.
    - **Open YNAB import** to bring in a supported YNAB export.
    - **Add manually** to track an account without a bank connection.

    See [Choose how to add your data](/getting-started/choose-your-first-data-path) if you are unsure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the account balance">
    Confirm that the balance and account type match your financial institution. Edit a manual account if its starting balance is wrong.

    <Warning>
      A linked balance can be delayed by the institution or data provider. Use your bank as the source of truth for time-sensitive decisions.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and categorize transactions">
    Open **Transactions**. Search for recent activity, assign categories to uncategorized items, and correct any transfer, income, refund, or expense that is classified incorrectly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your monthly budget">
    Open **Plan your money**, select **Monthly budget**, and add an amount for the categories you want to plan. Start with required bills and everyday spending; you can refine the plan later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a savings goal">
    Switch to **Savings goals** and create a goal for a longer-term priority such as an emergency fund or vacation. Add your current saved amount so progress starts from the right place.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose what happens next">
    Stay solo, or open **Settings → Household** to invite a partner. Joining a household does not make every account visible automatically; review each account in the **Privacy Center**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Check>
  Your setup is useful when your account totals look reasonable, recent transactions have categories, and the current month has a plan you understand.
</Check>

## Keep your plan current

- Review new transactions at least once a week.
- Check linked-account errors before relying on a balance.
- Update manual account balances when statements arrive.
- Revisit your monthly budget when income or bills change.
