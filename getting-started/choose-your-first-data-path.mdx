Miyo offers more than one way to start. You can use one path now and add another later.

| Path | Best when | What stays current automatically | What you maintain |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Connect Bank** | Your institution is available and bank sync is enabled for your account | Supported balances and transactions | Categories, notes, and any sync issues |
| **Import CSV** | You can export transactions from your bank | Nothing after the import | New files and the account balance |
| **YNAB import** | You are moving existing YNAB data into Miyo | Nothing after the import | New activity after the exported period |
| **Add manually** | You want full control or do not want to connect a provider | Nothing | Balance and transactions you enter |

## Connect Bank

Use this when you want supported account and transaction data to refresh through Plaid. Availability depends on Miyo access, your institution, and Plaid approval.

<Card title="Connect a bank" icon="landmark" href="/accounts/connect-a-bank">
  Review what Miyo requests and what to expect after linking.
</Card>

## Import CSV

Use a CSV when your bank can export transaction history but you do not want a persistent connection. Miyo can auto-detect common formats, and you can select a bank format when needed.

<Card title="Import a CSV" icon="file-up" href="/transactions/import-a-csv">
  Prepare a file and understand imported, duplicate, and skipped rows.
</Card>

## YNAB import

Use **Open YNAB import** from the first-data chooser when you are switching from YNAB. Review the import summary before treating Miyo as your new source of planning data.

## Add manually

Use a manual account for cash, an unsupported institution, a private balance you only want to maintain yourself, or a quick setup without transaction history.

<Tip>
  A small first setup is better than an incomplete full setup. Add the account you use most, review the result, then add the rest.
</Tip>
