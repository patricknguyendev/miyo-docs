The Debt workspace combines balances, minimum payments, due dates, and payoff scenarios. It is a planning tool; your lender remains the source of truth for balances, rates, and required payments.

## Add a debt

You can create a debt manually or choose **Import from connected account** when an eligible credit or loan account already exists in Miyo.

Enter or confirm:

- Current balance.
- Standard APR and any promotional APR end date.
- Minimum payment.
- Due day.
- Credit limit for utilization calculations.

<Tip>
  An imported account can supply balance context, but you may still need to add the minimum payment, due date, and APR from the latest statement.
</Tip>

## Choose a payoff strategy

| Strategy | Prioritizes | Tradeoff |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Avalanche** | Highest APR first | Often minimizes projected interest |
| **Snowball** | Smallest balance first | Creates earlier account payoffs |
| **Hybrid** | Quick wins plus APR | Balances momentum and interest cost |

Enter a **Monthly debt payment budget** at or above the total minimum payments. Miyo estimates a payoff date, order, suggested payment, and interest difference from a minimum-only scenario.

<Warning>
  Payoff results are projections, not lender quotes or financial advice. Variable rates, new purchases, fees, compounding methods, payment timing, and missing data can change the real outcome.
</Warning>

## Record a payment

Use the debt's payment action to record the amount and any available principal, interest, or fee detail. This updates Miyo's ledger and balance history.

<Note>
  Recording a debt payment in Miyo does not send money to the lender. Make the real payment through the lender or your bank.
</Note>

## Household privacy

When a debt is shared, your partner can see the due-date rhythm needed for household coordination. Miyo keeps private strategy details such as APR, utilization, promotional rates, notes, and payoff math out of the partner view.
