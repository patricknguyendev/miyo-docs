Account visibility controls whether an account and its activity participate in household views. Review it whenever you add an account or change household membership.

<Columns cols={3}>
  <Card title="Shared" icon="users">
    Household members can see the account and its activity in shared experiences.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Private" icon="lock-keyhole">
    Only you can see the account in your personal views.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Hidden" icon="eye-off">
    The account is excluded from dashboard totals, reports, and household views.
  </Card>
</Columns>

## Shared does not mean joint ownership

Sharing an account in Miyo changes visibility inside the product. It does not change legal ownership, bank permissions, beneficiaries, or the institution's records.

## Check before you share

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Privacy">
    Review the current visibility label beside each account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose intentionally">
    Consider the account balance, transaction history, and how its inclusion changes household totals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview the other view">
    Use the partner-view preview to confirm what another member will see.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the audit log">
    The Privacy Center records visibility changes so you can understand when a setting changed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  In solo mode, there is no other household member to share with. Your accounts remain visible only to you until you create or join a household and choose otherwise.
</Note>
